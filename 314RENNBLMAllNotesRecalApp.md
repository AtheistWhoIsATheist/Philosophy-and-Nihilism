- The core tenets of Nihiltheism include the primacy of nothingness, where the void is considered the ultimate reality and the source of all being, and the redefinition of the divine as the void, which transcends all human categories and conceptions.
- Nihiltheism employs an [Apophatic theology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apophatic_theology) approach, defining the divine through what it is not, rather than what it is, and utilizes a "language of [Silence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silence)" that acknowledges the limitations of language and conceptual thought in grasping ultimate reality.
- The framework critiques traditional theism for its belief in a personal [God](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/God) and an inherent meaning or purpose to the cosmos, and instead, reinterprets transcendence as a complete annihilation of the self, where one merges with the void.
- Nihiltheism recognizes the dialectical tension between theism and [Nihilism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nihilism) as a dynamic and essential aspect of the human condition, and explores the nature of the void through analogies from [Quantum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum) physics, such as [Quantum fluctuation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_fluctuation) and the holographic principle.
- The framework also draws parallels with mystical traditions, such as the concept of [Śūnyatā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Śūnyatā) in [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism), and utilizes neurophenomenological concepts, like the destabilization of the [Default mode network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Default_mode_network), to access a state of consciousness that is thought to be a "neurophenomenological singularity".
- [Existential crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Existential_crisis) is seen as a catalyst for transformation and a pathway to a deeper understanding of reality, and Nihiltheism calls for an "ethics of indifference", recognizing that all beings are equally insignificant and subject to the same fate.
- The framework includes a concept of "apophatic resonance", an experiential negation of immanent reality, and emphasizes the importance of continuous questioning, deconstruction, and paradoxical synthesis, always approaching the unfathomable without claiming to fully grasp it.
- Nihiltheism is not a philosophy of despair, but a way of living in the face of meaninglessness, a path of radical acceptance and freedom, and it utilizes [Apophatic theology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apophatic_theology) negation as an active process of questioning, not merely accepting a lack of knowledge, to ensure that it remains a dynamic process rather than a fixed doctrine.
- The framework rejects metaphysical absolutes and avoids reifying "nothingness" into a new metaphysical absolute, instead emphasizing the importance of continuous deconstruction and radical questioning, as seen in the concept of "despair amplification meditation" and the idea of "apophatic resonance", which can be induced through practices that destabilize the Default Mode Network.
